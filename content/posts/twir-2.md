This couple weeks I've read quite a lot. However nothing was so linked to software. 

- [Four thousand weeks](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/54785515-four-thousand-weeks) I can't recommend it enough. Yes, it is about time management, but with the fundational view of: your time is limited, you won't do everything. With that basis it has two ideas I like: (1) Time is just a sequence of moments - It's just the present (2) There are two freedoms: Fredom from ones' schedule and freedom to commit with others. 

- [You aren't responsible from everything that happens to you]([https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jcn1SlQyoTs](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jcn1SlQyoTs) You can only change what's under your control. Is a simple idea, but I like how he analizes the social media content that's been brocadsted about this *YOU* phenomena. 

- [Angents harness](https://www.philschmid.de/agent-harness-2026) All the software around the LLM **YOU CAN** and should control - Is a very intesrting concept because I feel like the development around agents in expansion mode, and not in optimization mode

- [Verifier's law](https://www.jasonwei.net/blog/asymmetry-of-verification-and-verifiers-law) Only problems that are easy to verify will be the ones that LLM's will be solve easily. You can't optimize what you can't measure well.
